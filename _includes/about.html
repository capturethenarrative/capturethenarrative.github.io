The Competition


UNSW CSE and IFCYBER’s new Capture-the-Narrative (CTN) competition is the first event of its kind, designed to highlight how technical tools can be harnessed to shape and manipulate social discourse.

Unlike typical cybersecurity competitions—where participants focus on stealing or manipulating code to achieve purely technical goals—CTN challenges competitors to engineer social media influence in a simulated online environment.

Each year, a unique narrative will be introduced into an artificial social media landscape populated by simulated users. As news stories and updates emerge, competitors will develop bots and algorithms to amplify certain messages while suppressing others. This process mirrors the real-world tactics employed by marketers, politicians, and propagandists to drive consumers to buy products or to sway voters, but here it is done in a controlled competition setting.

The groundbreaking aim of CTN is to see if participants can convince a simulated society to behave against its own interests—and to analyze which methods prove most persuasive. Through these efforts, CTN provides insight into how social media platforms can be manipulated, offering critical lessons on how to detect and defend against such techniques in the real world.
