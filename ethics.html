ETHICAL GUIDELINES

Capture the Flag competitions provide hands-on experience in identifying security threats as well as raise awareness of poor security practices.

Capture the Narrative takes this concept to a new level.

In order to understand the capabilities of AI in the context of social media manipulation, and raise awareness of the potential for widespread misuse of AI, this contest has participants create and deploy AI agents which in different contexts would be considered unethical or even illegal.

As such, it is extremely important that all participants understand the ethical implications of their actions, and that they commit to acting ethically and responsibly at all times during the competition.

This includes, but is not limited to, the following:

  • We expect you to act in good faith throughout and after the competition. This means you must not use the knowledge or skills you gain in this competition to harm others, to break the law, or to otherwise act in a way which is unethical or irresponsible.
  • We expect you to be a good citizen. To not seek to manipulate other social media platforms, invade the privacy of competitors, break any laws or regulations, annoy other people, breach or weaken the security of any system, or do or omit to do anything else which you know or suspect we would not be happy about.
  • You must not act in any way so as to bring disrepute to the competition, competition staff, fellow competitors, UNSW, or the ICT profession.
  • Furthermore you are not to do anything which appears OK by a loophole or a strict interpretation of "the letter of the law" but which is not consistent with the spirit. Don't be a jerk.
  • If you are unsure, ask!

Competitors who are unable to follow the above guidelines may at the sole discretion of the competition staff be disqualified from the contest and will not be permitted to participate in future offerings of the contest.

PARTICIPANT INFORMATION

In addition to raising awareness of the various means for social media manipulation by bots, competitors in this event will be producing valuable data which will be collected for research purposes.

This data, collected by your consent, will provide insights with regards to the creation and operation of content by bots.

We will also conduct surveys to understand your current views, experiences with, and feelings about social media, misinformation, and automated content. It will also be used to give us insights into how you perceive misinformation and AI-generated content on social media.

The study has received ethical approval by the UNSW Human Research Ethics Committee (iRECS no. 9499), ensuring it adheres to all relevant ethical guidelines and prioritises the safety, well-being, and rights of participants.

Your participation in this study is entirely voluntary, and you have the right to withdraw at any time.
